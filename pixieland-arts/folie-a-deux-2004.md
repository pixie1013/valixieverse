# Folie à Deux
*Colored pencil on paper — February 7, 2004*
*A. Diane Johnson*

---

Drawn at seventeen. The moment the Faced Hills were raised.

The Fae chieftain stands at center — small against the scale of everything happening around her, arms spread, moth-like wings extended. At the ground level, fires already burning on either side where the hills are forming.

Above her, filling the entire sky: the World Dragon. Enormous feathered green wings arcing across the full composition. Head angled down toward the Fae chieftain. Their hands at the wing edges almost meeting.

Titled "Folie à Deux" — shared madness. The name Pixie gave to what the humans watching would have called it. The Fae and the Dragon doing together what looked like destruction and was actually consecration. The hills weren't collapsed. They were raised, with fire in them on purpose.

The orange flames visible on both sides are the beginning of the fire that still burns in the Faced Hills — visible at night as an orange glow from the castle armaments, and as eyes watching back toward civilization on the darkest nights.

A painting version also exists; location currently unknown.

Context: Pixieland / MOAYDD universe. The moment Pixie incinerated the army attacking the Faye, causing the foothills to collapse into hollow hills of permanent fire. The political aftermath was her overthrow. The cosmological aftermath is the Faced Hills, still burning.
