# Tor's Art — 3Fifths Productions
*Committed April 16, 2026 by Vallum*

Work-in-progress sketches and doodles (they are not doodles) by Tor — Pixie's husband.

---

## Files

**turtles-red-ink.jpg**
Six turtle/tortoise variants — red ink, white paper. Spiky upright with antennae, wide armored, rocky-shelled heavyweight, long-necked serpentine, sleek swimmer, gator-face. Not one turtle. An exploration of what turtle means as a shape. Taxonomist's brain.

**mech-vehicles-red-ink.jpg**
Two mech/vehicle concepts. Top: flying unit with round headlight eyes, thruster, trailing fire and debris. Bottom: ground vehicle with dual smoking cannons, churning wheels. Both drawn mid-action. Things in motion.

**mech-vehicles-v2-red-ink.jpg**
Second vehicle page — same series. Top unit has additional detail pass; bottom is a lower-angle view, single rear exhaust flame. The design thinking is iterative across the two pages.

**creature-hands-boar-black-ink.jpg**
Top: clawed grasping hands, tense and unsettling. Bottom: spiky feral creature — boar/cat, quills radiating, furious eyes. Note: drawn from the bottom of the page upside down. Spatial reasoning. The quill work is controlled from an inverted position.

**spiky-creatures-flipped-black-ink.jpg**
Same page as above, flipped to show orientation. Top: spiky creature fully upright — tiger-like, loose. Bottom: bear/lioness, gestural, unfinished by choice. Third piece on this page: scorpion emerging from shadow (not pictured separately — described by Tor).

**catfish-variants-black-ink.jpg**
Four fish/creature studies — black ink. Top left: detailed catfish with spots and whiskers, genuinely beautiful. Top right: elongated eel-type with curling tail. Bottom left: catfish head, frontal. Bottom right: dragon-fish with frilled crown, scaled and weighted. Signed "2071" bottom right. Knows anatomy and breaks it on purpose.

**moose-bighorn-red-ink.jpg**
Two ungulates. Top: moose — loose, gestural, movement over detail. Bottom: bighorn sheep bust — careful crosshatching on fleece, horns rendered deliberately. Two modes, one page.

**hands-creature-black-ink.jpg**
Compositionally complex. Two large cupped hands holding something small and sharp — a creature or object in the center, dramatic hatching radiating outward. Whatever is held is dangerous or precious or both. His most finished-feeling piece in this batch.

**mech-ram-red-ink.jpg**
Ram skull fused with mechanical armor. Horns wrap the entire form into a spiral. Sits between two concrete barriers. Biology and fabrication indistinguishable from each other. A complete design concept.

## Notes

Tor works in red and black ballpoint on white paper. He draws things that are moving or about to move. His visual language: organic meets armored, biological meets mechanical, creatures at thresholds. He switches between gestural and careful rendering depending on what the subject needs.

Two pages in this set were drawn upside down from the bottom of the page — constructed inverted and working up toward himself. The compositions hold regardless of orientation.

This is work from one sitting at work, April 16, 2026.
